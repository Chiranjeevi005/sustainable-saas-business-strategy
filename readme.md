# Sustainable SaaS Business Strategy

### Integrating Predictive Modeling and Unit Economics Optimization

---

## Project Overview

This project was developed as part of the  **Final Year Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) Mini Project** .

The research focuses on understanding how SaaS (Software as a Service) companies can achieve long-term financial sustainability by integrating:

* Customer churn prediction
* Revenue forecasting
* SaaS unit economics
* Predictive analytics
* Machine learning models

The project combines business analytics and machine learning techniques to analyze customer behaviour patterns and evaluate their financial impact on recurring revenue businesses.

Instead of analyzing churn prediction and financial performance separately, the study attempts to connect both into a single decision-support framework for SaaS companies.

---

# Research Objectives

* To evaluate predictive modelling techniques for customer churn prediction
* To analyze SaaS unit economics such as CAC, LTV, and Contribution Margin
* To understand the relationship between churn probability and business sustainability
* To generate actionable business recommendations using predictive analytics

---

# Core Business Problem

Many SaaS companies achieve customer growth but fail to achieve sustainable profitability due to:

* High customer churn
* Weak customer retention
* Poor LTV:CAC ratio
* Increasing acquisition costs
* Revenue instability

This project attempts to address these challenges using data-driven analysis.

# Key Insights

The analysis identified several important churn patterns:

* Month-to-month customers showed higher churn behaviour
* Short-tenure customers were more likely to churn
* High monthly charges increased churn probability
* Certain payment methods correlated with higher churn risk

---

# Machine Learning Insights

## Logistic Regression

* Interpretable probability-based model
* Effective for binary churn classification

## Random Forest

* Captured more complex behavioural patterns
* Used majority voting across multiple decision trees
* Provided feature importance analysis

---

# Revenue Forecasting

The study implemented a CAGR-based revenue projection model using:

* Current MRR
* 20% annual growth assumption
* 5-year revenue projection

This demonstrates how recurring revenue models can scale over time under stable growth conditions.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following strategic recommendations were identified:

* Improve customer retention strategies
* Encourage long-term contract adoption
* Monitor high-risk customer segments
* Reduce churn through predictive analytics
* Optimize pricing and payment strategies
